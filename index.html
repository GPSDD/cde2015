Global Partnership for Sustainable Development Data (GPSDD)

Community Data Event

  • OpenGovHub 1110 Vermont Ave, Washington D.C.
  • Sept 18th - 19th

The SDG Community Data Event is a gathering of scientists, issue area experts, entrepreneurs, and last-mile users to discuss, plan, and build solutions that use data and public information to solve problems outlined in the Global Goals for Sustainable Development. Experts are gathering in person and virtually to share significant gaps in data that affect some of the most intractable problems of climate, gender, agriculture, and health. The diverse gathering will work together on strategies and partnerships to solve defined problems and unleash data to be used toward long term sustainable solutions.

The SDG Data Community Event is the first of a series of gatherings that will take place across the globe targeting each of the seventeen Global Goals in a lead-up to a summit on the Data Revolution planned for late Fall 2016. We hope you will join us and help identify, build, and partner on long term solutions to build a more just, equal, and empowered world.

About Us

The Global Partnership for the Sustainable Development Goals is an alliance of corporations, foundations, civil society organizations, non-profits, and governments that are working together to draw on the wealth of information and data that is increasingly available and has the capacity to achieve Global Goals goals faster, more effectively, and with long-term impact. We cannot do this alone. There are thousands of people from all backgrounds and experiences that are already working to use and share information to shape a better world. The Global Goals for Sustainable Development are setting an agenda to eliminate extreme poverty and continue the growth of a world with greater equality, individual agency, accountability, and collaboration across all peoples and sectors. The Global Partnership seeks to elevate these voices and grow a broader community committed to working together in transparent and inclusive collaboration to solve these longstanding problems.
